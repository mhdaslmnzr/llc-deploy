Kalaripayattu

Kalaripayattu
The ancient martial art style of Kerala, Kalaripayattu, is said to be the world's oldest and most scientific martial art. It has a history spanning more than three million years.The kalari is traditionally built by excavating a hole in the earth that is four feet deep, forty two feet long, and twenty-one feet wide. Typically, this is known as Kuzhikalari. In Malayalam, kuzhi is defined as "portions made by caving in the earth." This martial technique is practised in kuzhikalari at the Kadathanadan Kalari Center in Thekkady. A carefully thought-out gallery has been constructed so that spectators may see clearly from a safer distance. The unique mytho-historical history of Kerala gave rise to the ancient psycho-physiological discipline known as kalaripayattu, which is also a scientific method of physical culture instruction.
In contrast to other martial arts, Kalaripayattu's rigorous training emphasises the body's energy system as well as physical activity and agility. The body and mind are in complete harmony.
Kalaripayattu blends a variety of combat styles, including empty-handed combat, with a variety of weapons, such as long staffs, short sticks, curved sticks, swords and shields, spears, maces, and flexible swords (urumi). This ancient art form is characterised by suppleness and agility. The British during their rule in India went as far as to outlaw the practise of Kalaripayattu because they were threatened by its skill and potency. After then, the martial art was on the verge of extinction. Reviving this renowned art form of God's Own Country required the tireless efforts of a small group of committed individuals, including the late C.V. Narayanan Nair, Kottackal Kanaran, and others. Kalaripayattu is more than merely another martial art in Kerala. Instead, it is hard to distinguish between its origins and the State's cultural fabric since they are ingrained so deeply.

Kathakali

Kathakali
The story-telling dance genre known as kathakali originated in Kerala, a southern state. Kathakali derives from the words "Katha," which means "a narrative," and "Kali," which means "a dance or a performance." It is a hybrid art in which several actor-dancers play various parts. Usually, the tales are from the Ramayana and Mahabharata, two of the great Indian epics.
The clothes of kathakali show that a lot of aesthetic principles went into creating this art, which is incredibly grand. The idea is one of "whole theatre," in which singing, playing an instrument, colour psychology, and dress up all have unique roles to play.
Among all Indian traditional dance styles, "Kathakali" features the most elaborate makeup, costumes, face masks, headdresses, and colourfully painted faces. Its distinctive attire, accessories, and makeup, together with the magnificent performance, music, and lightings that bring the characters of the great epics and legends to life, draw in and astound both young and elderly audiences, creating an incredible atmosphere all around. Among the Indian traditional dance forms, the Kathakali dance form has the most intricate and vibrant makeup. The makeup, known as aharya-abhinaya, is based on the cultural tradition's colour psychology.The classic Kathakali performance starts in the evening and lasts all night, reaching its climax at the blessed hour of dawn, when Good ultimately triumphs over Evil. However, it has now been adapted for the proscenium stage, allowing urban spectators to partake in this ritualistic theatrical experience in the comfort of a luxurious auditorium in a matter of hours.

Jeep Safari

Jeep Safari
In Kerala's Pathanamthitta district is the hamlet of Gavi. Near Thekkady, it is about 14 kilometres from Kumily. Its spectacular natural splendour is found there and is a component of the Periyar Tiger Reserve. Beautiful fauna may be found in great abundance in the evergreen woods. For those who are interested in nature, Gavi provides a thorough introduction to the local flora, including Nageia Wallichinia (Podocarpus Wallichinia), a highly endemic species of the Gymnosperm family that is only found in the area surrounding Gavi in the entire Western Ghats.
The main off-the-beaten path location suited for a wilderness getaway with trekking, birding, canoeing, and boarding amenities is the jeep safari to Gavi.Many endangered animals, such as lion-tailed macaques, nilgiri tahrs, and other species, call this ecological garden, with its hills and valleys, tropical forests, expansive grasslands, sholas, flowing waterfalls, and cardamom plantation, home. After breakfast, a skilled guide leads a trek into the forest. Elephant Package

Elephant Package
One of the world's biggest land animals is the Indian elephant. The Asian elephant can make both delicate and forceful movements because to two finger-like appendages that are attached to the tip of its trunk. Elephants' bodies are covered with a scant layer of long, coarse hair. Their skin ranges from dark grey to brown. They have been tamed and utilised for transportation and the moving of large goods for thousands of years and have played a significant role in Asian culture. Indian elephants are highly social creatures that move from forest to forest, seldom remaining in one place for more than a few days..
